Slide 10 — AI Context (The Progression)

The five layers:

  • Chatbots — model can only talk
  • Tool Use — model can do things (APIs, files, shell). MCP is how the harness plugs tools in — "USB-C for AI tools"
  • Agents — model + tools + autonomous loop. They decide what to do next on their own
  • Agent Teams — multiple agents coordinating. The what and why (planning, reasoning)
  • Harness — everything surrounding the model. The how and where (executing tools, sandboxing, state)

Harness = Safe + Reliable

  • Safe (can't do) — controls what agents are NOT allowed to do. Can't delete production database, can't overspend, can't access files outside sandbox
  • Reliable (must do) — controls what agents MUST do. Retry on failure, preserve state, validate outputs

Analogy: agents are the workers. The harness is the building — walls, safety equipment, locked tool cabinets, fire exits.

Slide 14 — The Tool (Three Questions)

The three questions:

  • Q1 Who's making the call right now?
  • Q2 What am I about to do?
  • Q3 Does it serve the harness?

This is one tool within the self-harness system. You are the harness. Your modes are the agents. This tool gives you awareness of which one is driving.

It's not about making the "right" call — it's about being aware of who's making it. You have a feedback loop, like an agent's while loop. If it doesn't serve the goal, you'll know, and you can make a different call next time.

Slide 15 — Example: The Yes Trap

  • Q1: The people-pleaser is making the call
  • Q2: I'm about to say yes
  • Q3: It overloads everything else. Doesn't serve the harness

Slide 16 — Example: Networking

Three people at a table. Nobody looks up. That's terrifying for introverts.

  • Q1: The introvert is making the call
  • Q2: I'm about to walk away
  • Q3: If I show up, I get better at this. It serves the harness

Not saying you have to fake being someone you're not. Just an example of checking who's deciding.

Slide 17 — Example: Threshold Training

Threshold = push heart rate to sustainable limit for 30+ min. Most uncomfortable training a runner can do.

  • Q1: The tired runner is making the call
  • Q2: I'm about to jog back
  • Q3: Every other part of me gave up two hours for this — the coder, the worker, the one who could be enjoying life. The coach says: push. It serves the harness
